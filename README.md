# City St George's AI Research Day 2026

This repository contains the slides and contact details for the lightning talk **"Hackathons as a Medium for Disseminating Agentic AI in Research"** presented by Daniel Sikar and Dave Muir at **City St George's AI Research Day 2026**.

The talk looks at how hackathons can be used as a practical vehicle for introducing agentic AI into research, education, and workforce upskilling. It uses the **Clinical AI Hackathon** as a case study to show how hands-on, problem-led collaboration can move AI from abstract discussion into concrete research activity.

## Talk Summary

The presentation covers:

- how agentic AI emerged as a more operational workflow for research and technical work
- the shift from prompt-and-copy workflows to tool-using agents that can plan, execute, evaluate, and write up results
- why hackathons are a useful format for disseminating these workflows across research and teaching contexts
- lessons from a clinical hackathon built around a real NHS/MOD pain point

## Clinical AI Hackathon Case Study

The central case study in the talk is a hackathon focused on **structuring semi-structured medical notes**. Participants were given training and a baseline solution, then asked to improve it using AI agents.

Headline outcomes mentioned in the presentation:

- 76 sign-ups
- 32 finalists
- 9 teams
- 2 research placements generated at St George's NHS Foundation Trust

The presentation also highlights two follow-on research directions at St George's NHS Foundation Trust:

- monitoring radiology AI systems for performance drift over time
- evaluating subgroup bias to support fairer and safer deployment

## Why This Matters

The argument of the talk is that hackathons are not just outreach events. They can also function as a serious mechanism for:

- exposing researchers and students to agentic AI workflows
- building trust in generative and agent-based systems through applied use
- surfacing research questions that are immediately relevant to deployment and governance
- creating pathways from experimentation into placements, projects, and institutional adoption
